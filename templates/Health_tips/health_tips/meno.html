Managing Menopause Symptoms

Many treatments can help manage menopause symptoms, including prescription medications, alternative remedies, and certain lifestyle changes. The type of treatment that‚Äôs best for you can depend on your exact symptoms and health situation.

Menopause can bring a variety of symptoms, including hot flashes, vaginal dryness, and mood changes. Some people may be able to manage mild or moderate symptoms with nonprescription management strategies, while others may need help from a medical professional.

It can take some trial and error to find the treatment plan that works best for you. Before trying any alternative or natural remedies or lifestyle changes, talk with your doctor.

Hormone Therapy

Hormone therapy for menopause is one option to consider when your menopause symptoms are long lasting and more severe. It involves supplementing your levels of estrogen, progesterone, or both with medications.

Hormone therapy can come in many forms, including:

  • Tablets (Premarin, Prometrium)
  • Injection (Delestrogen)
  • Patches (Climara, Estraderm)
  • Skin creams
  • Gels
  • Vaginal creams
  • Vaginal rings
  • Subcutaneous (under the skin) pellets

Hormone therapy may help manage symptoms like hot flashes, night sweats, and vaginal dryness. For some people, it may also help lower the risk of complications like bone loss.

Bioidentical Hormones

Bioidentical hormones are lab-made hormones similar to natural hormones like estrogen, testosterone, and progesterone. They may help manage symptoms like hot flashes, night sweats, and mood changes.

Examples of FDA-approved bioidentical hormones include:

  • Estradiol (Vivelle)
  • Progesterone (Prometrium)

Nonhormonal Medications

Fezolinetant

Fezolinetant (Veozah) is a nonhormonal pill that may help people experiencing moderate to severe hot flashes.

Low Dose Antidepressants

Some low dose SSRIs and SNRIs, such as paroxetine (Brisdelle), may help relieve hot flashes, night sweats, and mood changes.

Gabapentin

Gabapentin (Neurontin), typically used for seizures, may help treat hot flashes off-label. It might have modest benefits but may not be as effective as estrogen.

Clonidine

Clonidine, a high blood pressure medication, may be used off-label to manage hot flashes, though it may be less effective than antidepressants or gabapentin.

Osteoporosis Medications

Menopause can lead to bone loss. Your doctor may recommend bone density testing, calcium and vitamin D supplements, or prescription medications such as:

  • Bisphosphonates (Alendronate - Fosamax)
  • Abaloparatide (Tymlos)
  • Raloxifene (Evista)
  • Calcitonin (Miacalcin)
  • Denosumab (Prolia)
  • Romosozumab (Evenity)

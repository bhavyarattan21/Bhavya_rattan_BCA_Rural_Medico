Migraine
Tips, tools, and support for living and thriving with migraine

Treating Migraine

To alleviate symptoms during an active migraine attack, several treatment options are used:

  • Pain Relievers: Over-the-counter (OTC) pain relievers like ibuprofen, aspirin, Excedrin, and acetaminophen can relieve mild to moderate migraine pain.
  • Triptans: For more severe migraine pain, stronger prescription medications such as triptans (e.g., sumatriptan, rizatriptan) are often used. Triptans help constrict blood vessels and block pain pathways in the brain.
  • Fioricet: A combination medication that helps relieve migraine symptoms through acetaminophen, butalbital, and caffeine.
  • Anti-nausea medications: Drugs like metoclopramide and prochlorperazine help alleviate nausea and vomiting during migraines.
  • Ergotamines: Medications like dihydroergotamine (DHE) in nasal spray or injection form, used when triptans are ineffective.
  • Corticosteroids: Short-term use of steroids like prednisone can help when migraines are resistant to other treatments.

Keep in mind that sometimes severe migraine pain responds well to OTC treatments, while mild symptoms might still need stronger medications like triptans or CGRP antagonists for relief.

Preventive Treatments for Migraine

Preventive treatments aim to reduce the frequency and intensity of migraine attacks and include:

  • Beta-blockers like propranolol
  • Anticonvulsants like topiramate and valproate
  • CGRP Inhibitors like erenumab and fremanezumab, blocking the CGRP protein involved in migraines
  • Botox injections (Botulinum toxin) approved for chronic migraine prevention

Treating Severe Migraine

Severe migraine episodes, characterized by intense, debilitating pain lasting days, require more intensive treatment strategies. These episodes often don't respond to OTC medications and significantly disrupt daily life.

The goal of treatment is to alleviate the acute pain, manage symptoms like nausea, and prevent future attacks.

For severe migraines:

  • Many treatments are similar to mild or moderate migraine treatments, but the response varies individually.
  • IV Medications: In emergency or hospital settings, intravenous (IV) treatments such as anti-nausea drugs (e.g., metoclopramide) and pain relievers (e.g., morphine or NSAIDs) are used for faster relief.

Identifying Your Hearing Issues

Buying hearing aids is rarely as easy as simply placing an online order. It‚Äôs recommended to first get a hearing test and consult a hearing specialist to determine which options may suit you best.

Having a Hearing Test

If you suspect hearing loss, it‚Äôs a good idea to have a diagnostic test. This provides an accurate measure of hearing loss and identifies any other problems, like wax buildup, infection, or injury.

You may also get referred to an otolaryngologist (ENT doctor) if you have:

  • Unusual patterns of hearing loss
  • Asymmetrical (uneven) hearing loss
  • Pain or drainage
  • Vertigo

Even if buying an online device, it‚Äôs important to know the nature and severity of your hearing loss to select the right device.

Talking with a Specialist About Your Options

People often underestimate their hearing loss. A specialist can properly diagnose the issue and recommend solutions. Audiologists are trained to diagnose and treat hearing disorders and can suggest hearing aids, auditory training, or medical treatments like cochlear implants if necessary.

Should I See a Hearing Specialist?

If you notice trouble hearing, contacting a hearing specialist is a smart first step. They can test your hearing, recommend treatment, and refer you to an ENT doctor if needed.

Finding the Right Type of Hearing Aid

Hearing aids vary greatly ‚Äî some fit behind the ear, some inside the ear canal. Some connect to your phone, reduce background noise, or use rechargeable batteries.

Types of Hearing Aids

Getting a professional assessment helps you choose a device that:

  • Matches your hearing loss type
  • Fits your lifestyle
  • Fits your budget
  • Is comfortable to wear

Main types of hearing aids:

  • Behind-the-ear (BTE)
  • In-the-canal (ITC) or completely in-the-canal (CIC)
  • In-the-ear (ITE)
  • Receiver-in-canal (RIC)

Each type has pros and cons, depending on visibility, airflow, and comfort.

Special Features

Hearing aids are more than just amplifiers. Many offer features like:

  • Background noise filtering
  • Smartphone connectivity
  • Different levels of visibility
  • Rechargeable batteries
  • Enhanced comfort

How Do I Choose the Right Type of Hearing Aid?

Hearing aids vary in design, fit, and features. To find what fits your needs and lifestyle best, consult a hearing specialist who can guide you with personalized recommendations.

What is First Aid?

First aid is the immediate assistance given to a person suffering from a minor or serious illness or injury before professional medical help is available. It can save lives, prevent conditions from worsening, and promote recovery.

Why is First Aid Important?

  • Provides quick and effective response in emergencies.
  • Reduces recovery time.
  • Can prevent a situation from becoming worse.
  • Promotes confidence and safety awareness.

Golden Rules of First Aid

  1. Stay calm and assess the situation.
  2. Call emergency services immediately if needed.
  3. Protect yourself and the injured person from danger.
  4. Do not move a person with major injuries unless necessary.
  5. Stop any bleeding with pressure and a clean cloth.
  6. Keep the injured person warm and comfortable.
  7. Reassure them and never leave them alone if possible.

First Aid Kit Essentials

  • Adhesive bandages (various sizes)
  • Antiseptic wipes and cream
  • Sterile gauze and tape
  • Scissors and tweezers
  • Gloves (non-latex)
  • Thermometer
  • Pain relievers (paracetamol, ibuprofen)
  • CPR mask or face shield
  • Emergency contact numbers card

  • Bleeding: Apply direct pressure, elevate the wound.
  • Choking: Perform abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver).
  • Fainting: Lay the person down, raise their legs slightly.
  • Fracture: Immobilize the limb, do not move it.
  • Heart Attack: Call emergency, give aspirin if conscious.

Preventing Heart Disease

Exercising, eating a well-balanced diet, and managing your blood pressure are some of the ways to help you prevent heart disease.

Heart disease refers to a group of conditions that affect your heart, some of which include:

  • Coronary heart disease
  • Heart failure
  • Arrhythmia

Heart disease is the leading cause of death in the United States, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). In 2021, it was responsible for 1 in 5 deaths.

Risk Factors

Two types of risk factors could increase your risk of heart disease:

  • Non-modifiable risk factors are those you can‚Äôt control, such as genetics.
  • Modifiable risk factors are those you can control, including:
    • Smoking
    • High blood pressure
    • High cholesterol

Ways to Reduce Risk

Quit Smoking

Smoking is one of the biggest risk factors for heart disease. It causes plaque buildup in the arteries, damages organs, reduces good cholesterol, and increases blood pressure.

Quitting smoking can greatly help reduce your risk. Speak with a healthcare professional to develop a cessation program and build a support network.

Nutrition and Diet

Diet plays a key role in preventing heart disease. Even with a family history, eating the right foods can help lower your risk.

Physical Activity and Weight Management

Exercising and maintaining a healthy weight can lower your risk of heart disease by:

  • Strengthening heart muscles
  • Lowering blood pressure
  • Improving leptin and insulin sensitivity
  • Lowering plasma-lipid levels
  • Decreasing blood thickness
  • Relaxing blood vessels

The American Heart Association recommends at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise or 75 minutes of high-intensity exercise per week.

Even light activities like walking and gardening are beneficial.

Managing Diabetes

Diabetes significantly increases your risk of heart disease. Management includes:

  • Taking medications
  • Monitoring blood sugar, blood pressure, and cholesterol
  • Eating a balanced diet
  • Exercising regularly
  • Getting regular checkups

Lowering Blood Pressure

High blood pressure forces your heart to work harder, increasing the risk of heart disease. Ways to manage blood pressure include:

  • Following a treatment plan
  • Eating a low-fat, balanced diet
  • Exercising and maintaining weight
  • Lowering stress levels
  • Quitting smoking
  • Limiting salt and alcohol intake

Managing Stress

High levels of stress over time may contribute to heart disease. Stress can affect your brain, immune system, nervous system, and vascular system.

Ways to reduce stress include:

  • Practicing mindfulness activities like yoga, meditation, and breathing techniques
  • Exercising
  • Eating a balanced diet
  • Minimizing screen time

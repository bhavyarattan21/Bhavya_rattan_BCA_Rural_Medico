Exposure Therapy for Anxiety

If you‚Äôre living with a specific phobia, exposure therapy can be an effective treatment for managing your anxiety.

Exposure therapy is a kind of behavioral therapy that healthcare professionals typically use to help people living with phobias and anxiety disorders. It involves a person facing what they fear, either imagined or in real life, but under the guidance of a trained therapist in a safe environment. Healthcare professionals can use exposure therapy with people of all ages, and research has found it to be effective.

Learning more about exposure therapy can help you make an informed decision about treatment and prepare you for what to expect.

How Exposure Therapy Works

In exposure therapy, a therapist exposes a person to a situation, event, or object that triggers anxiety, fear, or panic for them. Over time, controlled exposure in a safe space can lessen anxiety or panic.

Different kinds of exposure therapies include:

  • In vivo exposure: Directly facing the feared situation or activity in real life.
  • Imaginal exposure: Vividly imagining the trigger situation in detail.
  • Virtual reality exposure: Useful when in vivo exposure isn‚Äôt realistic, such as fear of flying.
  • Interoceptive exposure: Purposefully triggering a feared but harmless physical sensation.

Within these therapies, different techniques include:

  • Prolonged exposure (PE): Combining in vivo and imaginal exposure to revisit traumatic events and gain new perspectives.
  • Exposure and response prevention (EX/RP or ERP): Typically used for OCD, involving exposure and preventing compulsive responses.

Generalized Anxiety

Treatment for generalized anxiety disorder (GAD) can include imaginal and in vivo exposure, but in vivo is less common. Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) and imaginal exposure have shown improved functioning compared with relaxation and nondirective therapy.

Social Anxiety

Healthcare professionals typically use in vivo exposure for social anxiety, such as attending social events and facing avoided activities. Exposure with or without cognitive therapy may help reduce symptoms.

Driving Anxiety

Virtual reality exposure therapy has helped reduce driving anxiety, though more research is needed. Other therapies may also be necessary.

Public Speaking

Virtual reality exposure therapy has been effective in treating public speaking anxiety. A small 2020 study found decreased anxiety after a 3-hour VR session, with benefits lasting for 3 months.

Separation Anxiety

Exposure therapy is considered the top treatment for separation anxiety disorder in children. It involves exposure to feared situations while encouraging adaptive behavior and thinking.

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

ERP therapy, combining imaginal and in vivo exposure, is often used for OCD. It‚Äôs most effective when guided by a therapist and when combining both exposure types rather than just in vivo exposure.

Panic Disorder

Interoceptive exposure therapy is often used for panic disorder. Face-to-face sessions with professionals are associated with better effectiveness and treatment acceptance.

How Effective Is It?

Exposure therapy is effective for treating anxiety disorders. According to EBBP.org, about 60% to 90% of people have either no symptoms or only mild symptoms after completing exposure therapy.

Combining exposure therapy with CBT, relaxation techniques, and other treatments may further enhance its effectiveness.

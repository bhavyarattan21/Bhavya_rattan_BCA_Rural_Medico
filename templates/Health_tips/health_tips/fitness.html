1. Lunges

Challenging your balance is an essential part of a well-rounded exercise routine. Lunges promote functional movement while increasing strength in your legs and glutes.

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and arms at your sides.
  • Step forward with your right leg and bend your right knee until your thigh is parallel to the ground.
  • Push up off your right foot and return to the starting position. Repeat with your left leg.
  • Complete 3 sets of 10 reps.

2. Pushups

Pushups are one of the most basic, yet effective bodyweight moves because of the number of muscles recruited.

  • Start in a plank position with core tight and neck neutral.
  • Bend elbows to lower your body, then push back to start.
  • Complete 3 sets of as many reps as possible.

3. Squats

Squats increase lower body and core strength while improving flexibility in the lower back and hips.

  • Stand straight, feet slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  • Brace core, push hips back, bend knees as if sitting in a chair.
  • Drop until thighs are parallel to the ground, then return.
  • Complete 3 sets of 20 reps.

4. Standing Overhead Dumbbell Presses

This compound exercise works your shoulders, upper back, and core.

  • Use 10-pound dumbbells, stand with feet shoulder-width apart.
  • Push weights overhead until arms are fully extended.
  • Lower back down and repeat.
  • Complete 3 sets of 12 reps.

5. Dumbbell Rows

Dumbbell rows strengthen multiple muscles in your upper body.

  • Hold a dumbbell in each hand, bend forward at the waist.
  • Pull one weight up toward your chest, then lower it.
  • Alternate arms. Complete 3 sets of 10 reps.

6. Single-Leg Deadlifts

Single-leg deadlifts improve balance and work hamstrings and glutes.

  • Hold a dumbbell and hinge at hips, kicking one leg back.
  • Lower the weight toward the ground, then return.
  • Complete 3 sets of 10-12 reps per side.

7. Burpees

Burpees offer a whole-body workout improving cardiovascular endurance and strength.

  • Squat, place hands on ground, jump back to pushup position.
  • Jump feet forward, stand, and jump up.
  • Complete 3 sets of 10 reps.

8. Side Planks

Side planks strengthen your core and improve spine stability.

  • Lie on one side, prop up with forearm, stack legs.
  • Lift hips to form a straight line, hold, and lower.
  • Complete 3 sets of 10‚Äì15 reps on each side.

9. High Planks

High planks engage abs, shoulders, triceps, glutes, and quads without straining your back.

  • Start in pushup position with hands under shoulders.
  • Hold position with core tight for 30 seconds.
  • Complete 2-3 sets.

10. Glute Bridge

Glute bridges strengthen your entire posterior chain and improve posture.

  • Lie on the floor, knees bent, feet flat, arms at sides.
  • Push through heels to lift hips, squeeze glutes at top.
  • Lower back down. Complete 3 sets of 10‚Äì12 reps.

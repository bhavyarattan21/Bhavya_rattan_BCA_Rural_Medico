Weight Management
Tips, tools, and support for sustainable weight management

Prescription Weight Loss Medications

Prescription weight loss medications, including GLP-1 agonists, orlistat, and Contrave, may be effective for some people. But other lifestyle changes are still necessary for long-term success.

Currently Approved Weight Loss Medications

The FDA has approved several drugs for losing weight with overweight and obesity. These include:

  • GLP-1 agonists (liraglutide, semaglutide, tirzepatide)
  • Orlistat (Xenical and Alli)
  • Phentermine/topiramate (Qsymia)
  • Naltrexone/bupropion (Contrave)
  • Setmelanotide (Imcivree)
  • Appetite suppressants (phentermine)

These medications must be combined with a balanced diet and medical supervision. Possible side effects may occur.

How Effective Are Prescription Weight Loss Drugs?

Prescription medications can help you lose 5% to 10% of body weight. However, for best results, they should be used with lifestyle changes such as diet and exercise to maintain weight loss after discontinuation.

Pros and Cons of Weight Loss Medications

Pros

  • Effective for weight loss with healthy lifestyle changes
  • FDA-approved for weight management
  • May improve blood sugar, blood pressure, and cholesterol
  • Available through telehealth platforms

Cons

  • Not suitable for everyone (certain conditions or eating disorders)
  • Possible medication interactions
  • Not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding
  • Potentially expensive without insurance
  • Possible serious side effects
  • Weight regain after stopping medication
  • More long-term research needed

GLP-1 Agonists

Three GLP-1 agonists approved for weight loss:

  • Liraglutide (Saxenda)
  • Semaglutide (Wegovy)
  • Tirzepatide (Zepbound)

Semaglutide was also approved to reduce heart disease risks in overweight adults. These medications are injectable, either daily (liraglutide) or weekly (semaglutide and tirzepatide).

Other GLP-1 agonists used off-label:

  • Semaglutide (Ozempic, Rybelsus)
  • Dulaglutide (Trulicity)
  • Liraglutide (Victoza)
  • Exenatide (Byetta, Bydureon BCise)
  • Tirzepatide (Mounjaro)

How It Works

GLP-1 agonists slow stomach emptying, increase feelings of fullness, and reduce appetite-related hormone secretion. Tirzepatide binds to both GLP-1 and GIP receptors for an enhanced effect.

Cost

GLP-1 medications cost between $855 and $1,349.02 per month, depending on the brand, dosage, and pharmacy. Insurance coverage may lower the cost.

Effectiveness

A study of 1,961 adults showed that weekly semaglutide injections led to nearly 15% body weight loss after 68 weeks. Another 2023 study found tirzepatide users lost 20% or more of their weight over 72 weeks.
